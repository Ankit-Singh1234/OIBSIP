Tribute to Flying Officer Nirmal Jit Singh Sekhon

Nirmal Jit Singh Sekhon

Born: 17 July 1943, Ludhiana, Punjab, British India
Died: 14 December 1971, Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir, India
Allegiance: Republic of India
Service/Branch: Indian Air Force
Rank: Flying Officer
Unit: No. 18 Squadron
Awards: Param Vir Chakra (posthumous)

About Nirmal Jit Singh Sekhon

Nirmal Jit Singh Sekhon was a valiant officer of the Indian Air Force, born in a Sikh Jat family in Ludhiana, Punjab. He joined the Indian Air Force in 1967 and was known for his exceptional skills as a pilot. During the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971, Sekhon displayed remarkable bravery while defending the Srinagar Air Base against a Pakistani air raid.

On 14 December 1971, despite being outnumbered, Sekhon single-handedly engaged six enemy aircraft, displaying unparalleled courage and skill. Though his aircraft was eventually shot down, his actions prevented further damage to the base. He was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra, India’s highest military decoration for gallantry in the face of the enemy.

Honors and Recognition

Flying Officer Nirmal Jit Singh Sekhon's bravery is commemorated with several statues across Punjab and at the Indian Air Force Museum in Palam. His legacy continues to inspire countless individuals in India and beyond. A marine tanker built in 1985 was also named in his honor. His story is a testament to the spirit and dedication of the Indian Armed Forces.
